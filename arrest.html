[Getting Arrested]

When traveling, be aware of the political and social climate of the country. Many African countries are known for corruption that everyday citizens can witness and experience first hand. Corruption is abundant in most countries, especially in the United States; however, it is not something we blatantly witness firsthand and often. It is very uncommon to have to bribe anyone to let you enter/exit an airport. With this, be very careful and remain calm in situations, which may involve the law or justice systems. Do not, act like you have no sense and carry on like you can do anything. If you can, do not give bribe money. Lastly, if you have to, pull the “I am alerting the Embassy on ya” card.

If you do get arrested.....

Keep your calm. I can't stress this enough, an aggrevated will not help your situation. You will typically be taken into custody and held in a cell for questioning. You do however have the right to...
  1. Get free legal advice
  2. Tell someone where you are
  3. Have medical help if you’re feeling ill
  4. See the rules the police must follow
  5. See a written notice telling you about your rights, eg regular breaks for food and to use the toilet (you can ask for a notice in your language) or an interpreter to explain the notice
